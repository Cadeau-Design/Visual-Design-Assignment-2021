Christ, the Redeemer is an Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ and the most iconic image of Rio de Janeiro.

1) The Real Christ

The statue of Christ, the Redeemer, symbolises Rio de Janeiro and it is widely recognised as one of the biggest Art Deco statues of its kind and as I am experimenting with nostalgia, I am manipulating it, by using different colours and applying the concept of complementary and analogous colours.

The original image was download from Shutterstock, which I have imported to Photoshop. For this work, I have selected the subject, in this case, the Christ, by using the 'Object Selection Tool' and then I have manipulated the colour balance by experimenting with the mid tones.

Once I was happy with the result of the image I have exported it as a 'Save for Web (legacy)'.

Final file information: exported as PNG24, dimensions: 1080 x 1080 pixels, Bit depth 24 and size: 1.60mb.

Original file information: downloaded as JPEG, dimensions: 5121 x 3414 pixels and 300 DPI.

2) Christ, The Redeemer as pop art

In this section, continuing with the theme of nostalgia, I was inspired by the Pop Art movement that emerged during the 1950s.

I have drawn inspiration, specifically, from the work that Andy Warhol did with images of the iconic American actress Marilyn Monroe.

I have selected the subject, in this case, the Christ, by using the 'Object Selection Tool', then copy the selection into a new canvas, then manipulating the background colour and Christ's colour itself.

I have then applied colours based on the colour theory, experimenting with the analogous and complimentary colours. My main aim in this experiment was to understand how colours can compliment themselves or shock the audience with different vibrant colours.

Final file information: exported as PNG-24, dimensions: 1080 x 1080 pixels, Bit depth 24 and size: 418 KB.

3) Copacabana Beach, Rio de Janeiro and its pavement

Copacabana is perhaps the most iconic neighbourhood of Rio de Janeiro, it is also a 'Balneario' beach which stretches for 4 km (2.5 miles).

The promenade is parallel to the Beach and its zigzag wave pattern is fashioned from black and white tiles designed by Roberto Burle Marx in 1970 who was inspired by the 'calçadas Portuguesas' widely seen in Lisbon, especially in the Rossio Square.

For this experiment, I have downloaded the images from Shutterstock and imported them into Photoshop. By using a different range of tools, such as 'Quick Selection Tool' and 'Magic Wand Tool', I was then able to select different parts of the image and apply the colour balance adjustment option. By doing this, I was able to adjust the colour balance and temperature of the light exposure in the image.

My main aim with this experiment was to manipulate the image and create a feeling of 'hot' and 'cold' temperatures, enhance shadows and apply a stronger contrast between colours.

4) Cais das Colunas, Lisbon

The Cais das Colunas is located at the Praça do Comércio, central Lisbon, on the famous Tagus River. This is a place of peace and beauty, where people gather to enjoy the sun and the waterfront.

It reminds me of my home country, Brazil, and especially the historic parts of Rio de Janeiro, which was the home to the Portuguese Royal family from 1808 until 1821, when the Portuguese King went back to Portugal.

Therefore, the two cities, Rio de Janeiro and Lisbon have a lot of similarities, such as architecture and urbanisation which was heavily developed during the period that Rio de Janeiro was the capital of the Kingdom of Portugal.

As Cais das Colunas has a special place in my memories when I visit Lisbon, I wanted to include them as an image in this project, as another symbol of nostalgia and melancholy.

For this part of the experiment, I have followed the same technique that I used for part three of the Image Portfolio, I downloaded the images from Shutterstock and imported them into Photoshop. By using a different range of tools, such as 'Quick Selection Tool' and 'Magic Wand Tool', I was then able to select different parts of the image and apply the colour balance adjustment option. By doing this, I was able to adjust the colour balance and temperature of the light exposure in the image.

My main aim with this experiment was also to play with colours, enhance shadows and apply different colours from the original image.
